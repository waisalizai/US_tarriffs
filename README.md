# Global Inflation and Trade Wars Analysis
## Overview
This project, submitted to Sandbox programme at the Goldsmiths, University of London on 9 April 2025, analyses the global economic impact of US trade tariffs under President Trump, focusing on inflation, UK exports, and monetary policy responses. The study includes data visualisations and forecasts to illustrate effects on key industries.
## Motivation
Understanding the consequences of trade conflicts is crucial for policymakers, businesses, and consumers. This project demonstrates potential short- and long-term economic impacts using real-world data.

## Key Findings
- US tariffs contribute to global inflation and affect UK exports.
- Luxury car prices could rise significantly due to tariff pass-through.
- Monetary policy, such as Bank of England rate hikes, is partially counteracting inflation.
- Some firms may relocate to the UK to avoid tariffs, creating potential investment opportunities.
